Hello! 👋

I am a first-year PhD student at the University of Maryland Vision and Learning Lab, where I am lucky to be advised by Professor Jia-Bin Huang. In addition to computer vision and deep learning research, I have also worked on problems in visual psychophysics under the guidance of Professor Ralf Haefner and Professor Greg DeAngelis to study human perception of moving objects during ego motion.

I am very intrigued by human × machine intelligence. In particular, I am interested in human-inspired (or even more broadly, bio-inspired) perception, 3D vision, and multimodal learning, inspired by the fact that, while humans depend most heavily on vision to perceive, other modalities such as hearing, touch, and vestibular cues are also essential to how we interpret the world around us. I am also interested in devising self-supervised learning or few-shot learning methods to incorporate insights from humans’ abilities to generalize with little curated data and to learn quickly and continuously. As such, my goal is to leverage multisensory information to help embodied agents not only to perceive – but also to understand and reason about – the 3D world better.
